TERMS OF USE.

The terms of use constitute the plagiarism policy and the liability(new name) policy. Choosing to be a mentor or mentee entails that the plagiarism policy and the liability policy are agreed to. We require these are read before a decision to join UNi-ED is made and a decision to join UNi-ED as a mentor or mentee entails an agreement to abide by these policies.

Plagiarism Policy

UNi-ED aims to provide a tool, in the form of mentors, to help high school students strengthen their understanding of a country’s application systems, colleges and financial aid opportunities. Using this augmented understanding, HSS are required to complete their applications using their own abilities.

At no point during the mentorship process may any mentee plagiarise any material the mentor shares with them and use it towards their own application. This clearly violates the rules of the college application process and UNi-ED’s ethos. Plagiarism of any intellectual property that a mentor may share is strongly condemned by UNi-ED.

Additionally, UNi-ED mentors must not, at any point, directly edit any aspect of the mentee’s application portfolio; this includes all material required to complete an application any university.

Doing so would violate the terms of use for UNi-ED. Upon becoming aware of plagiarism, a mentor is required to report this to UNi-ED.

  • By agreeing to be a mentor, the college student agrees to abide by the aforementioned, including reporting any plagiarism and not facilitating plagiarism.
  • By agreeing to be a mentee, the individual agrees to abide by the aforementioned, including not plagiarising any intellectual property a mentor may share for inspiration.
